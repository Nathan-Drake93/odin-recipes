Homemade Hamburger Helper

Description

This homemade take on the classic Hamburger Helper beef pasta is hearty, creamy, and packed with flavor. Tender ground beef is simmered with savory seasonings, pasta, and a rich, cheesy sauce to create the ultimate comfort food. It's an easy one-pan dinner that comes together quickly, tastes even better than the boxed version, and is fully gluten-free so the whole family can enjoy it.

Ingredients

  • 2 Tbsp Olive Oil
  • 1 Medium Diced Yellow Onion
  • 3 Cloves Garlic
  • 1 LB Ground Beef
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • 6 oz Tomato Paste
  • 1 Tsp Chili Powder
  • 4 Cups Beef Stock
  • 8 oz Gluten Free Farfalle or Mafalda Pasta (I use Jovial)
  • 1/3 Cup Sour Cream
  • 1 1/2 Cups Shredded Mild Cheddar Cheese

Instructions

  1. Heat a large pot over medium heat, add the olive oil and heat through.
  2. Add the onion and sauté until the onion is soft and translucent, about 6 minutes.
  3. Add the garlic and continue to sauté until the garlic is fragrant, about 30 seconds.
  4. Add the ground beef. Breaking it up and cooking until the meat is browned, about 8 minutes. Season with salt and pepper.
  5. Stir in the tomato paste and chili powder.
  6. Add the beef stock and bring to a boil.
  7. Add the pasta, cover, and cook, stirring occasionally, until the pasta is al dente, about 8 to 10 minutes (the cooking time may differ depending on the pasta shape).
  8. Remove from heat and stir in the sour cream, and cheddar cheese. Stirring until the cheese has melted and everything is incorporated.
  9. Serve immediately.

Notes

  • Corn-based pasta doesn't work as well in this recipe. The flavor and texture can be off. Stick with rice-based pasta for the best results.
  • If using beef broth, add 1 tsp beef bouillon to boost the richness of the sauce.
